      <h3>April Term 1816 —</h3>
      
      
      <h4>Revd 
         <span class="persName">John Ashton</span>. Will —
      </h4>
      
      
      <p>In the Name of God the Father son and Holy Ghost Amen. I 
         <span class="persName">John
            Ashton
         </span> of 
         <span class="placeName">Charles County</span> in the State of
         
         <span class="placeName">Maryland</span> being of sound memory and understanding do
         constitute and make this my last will and testament evoking all wills made prior
         to the date hereof—
      </p>
      
      
      <p>Imprimis. I bequeath my soul to God in whose mercies I place all my hopes and my
         body to the earth to be decently intered at St Thomas's burying Ground with a
         solemn Mass of Requiem—
      </p>

      <h4>April Term 1816 —</h4>
      
      
      <p>Item     I bequeath to my Good friend the Reverend 
         <span class="persName">Nottey
            Young
         </span> the following tracts of land lying in 
         <span class="placeName">Charles
            County
         </span> near 
         <span class="placeName">Port Tobacco</span> and now in my
         possession vizt A tract of land called 
         <span class="placeName">Chandlers Hills</span>
         where I now live containing two Hundred acres of land more or less; A tract of
         land called 
         <span class="placeName">St. Nicholas</span> containing two Hundred acres of
         land more or less; a tract of land called 
         <span class="placeName">Green a</span>
         containing one Hundred acres of land more or less; a tract of land called
         
         <span class="placeName">little worth</span> containing one Hundred and sixty four acres
         more or less; a tract of land called 
         <span class="placeName">Small Profit</span>
         containing one Hundred and thirty six acres more or less; The unsold parts of
         two tracts of land called 
         <span class="placeName">Campbells Chance</span> and
         
         <span class="placeName">Struthbrant</span> lying on 
         <span class="placeName">Elk ridge</span> in
         
         <span class="placeName">Ann arundel County</span> amounting to about two Hundred and
         fifty acres by conjecture and now for sale. I bequeath to a youth called
         
         <span class="persName">Charles Queen</span> and to his sister 
         <span class="persName">Elizabeth
            Queen
         </span> the children of 
         <span class="persName">Susanna Queen</span> the
         unalienated part of a tract of land called 
         <span class="placeName">Litchfield
            Enlarged
         </span> lying in 
         <span class="placeName">Charles County</span> whereon
         they now live with all the stock of Horses, Hogs, Cattle, and utensils of every
         kind found thereon at my death together with the Tobacco Grain and Property of
         every description. I also give to the said 
         <span class="placeName">Charles</span> a
         negro Man called 
         <span class="persName">Butler</span> and the Male children of a Negro
         woman called 
         <span class="persName">Linny</span> and I give to his sister
         
         <span class="persName">Elizabeth</span> the negro woman called 
         <span class="persName">Linny</span>
         with her other children. I give to the aforesaid 
         <span class="persName">Nottey
            Young
         </span> all the property which I hold on the books of the United
         States loan office, Bank stock, Baltimore insurance office by Note or by Bond
         charging him with the payment of all my debts and contracts. I give and bequeath
         to the same 
         <span class="persName">Nottey Young</span> all other real personal or mixed
         Estate of which I may be possessed at my death charging him with the
      </p>
