            <h3>DEDICATORY</h3>
            
            <h4>To R.C.C. 
               <span class="smallcaps">AND</span> C.D.C.
            </h4>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Somewhere, sometime, in an April twilight,</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>When the hills are hid in violet shadows</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>When meadow brooks are still and hushed for wonder,</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>At the ring dove's call as at a summons,</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Let us gather from the world's fair quarters,</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Stealing from the trackless dusk like shadows,</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Meet to wait the moon, and greet in silence.</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>When she swims above the April branches,</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Rises clear of naked oak and beeches,</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Sit with me beneath the snowy orchard,</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Where the white moth hangs with wings entranced,</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Drunken with the still perfume of blossoms.</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Then, for that the moon was ours of olden,</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Let it work again its old enchantment.</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Let it, for an April night, transform us</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>From our grosser selves to happy shadows</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Of the three who lay and planned at moonrise,</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>On an island in a western river,</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Of the conquest of the world together.</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Let us pour our amber wine and drink it</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>To the memory of our vanished kingdom,</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>To our days of war and ocean venture,</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Brave with brigandage and sack of cities;</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>To the Odysseys of summer mornings,</em>
                
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               
               <em>Starry wonder-tales of nights in April.</em>
                
            </div>
             
         </div>

   <div class="lg poem">
      
      <h4>"GRANDMITHER, THINK NOT I FORGET"</h4>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">Grandmither, think not I forget, when I come back to
            <br></br>town, 
         </div>
         
         <div class="l">An' wander the old ways again an' tread them up an'
            <br></br>down. 
         </div>
         
         <div class="l">I never smell the clover bloom, nor see the swallows
            <br></br>pass, 
         </div>
         
         <div class="l">Without I mind how good ye were unto a little lass. </div>
         
         <div class="l">I never hear the winter rain a-pelting all night through, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Without I think and mind me of how cold it falls on you. </div>
         
         <div class="l">And if I come not often to your bed beneath the thyme, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Mayhap 't is that I'd change wi' ye, and gie my bed for
            <br></br>thine, 
         </div>
         
         <div class="l indented2">Would like to sleep in thine. </div>
          
      </div>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">I never hear the summer winds among the roses blow, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Without I wonder why it was ye loved the lassie so. </div>
         
         <div class="l">Ye gave me cakes and lollipops and pretty toys a score,— </div>
         
         <div class="l">I never thought I should come back and ask ye now for
            <br></br>more. 
         </div>
         
         <div class="l">Grandmither, gie me your still, white hands, that lie
            <br></br>upon your breast, 
         </div>
         
         <div class="l">For mine do beat the dark all night and never find me
            <br></br>rest; 
         </div>
         
         <div class="l">They grope among the shadows an' they beat the cold
            <br></br>black air, 
         </div>
         
         <div class="l">They go seekin' in the darkness, an' they never find him
            <br></br>there, 
         </div>
         
         <div class="l indented2">As They never find him there. </div>
          
      </div>
      
      <span class="hr"> </span>
      <span class="pageimage">
         <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/large/cat.0005.014.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/large/cat.0005.014.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;">
            <img src="http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/thumbnails/cat.0005.014.jpg" class="display"></img>
         </a>
      </span>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">Grandmither, gie me your sightless eyes, that I may
            <br></br>never see 
         </div>

      <h4>ASPHODEL</h4>
      
      <div class="l">As some pale shade in glorious battle slain, </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">On beds of rue, beside the silent streams, </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">Recalls outworn delights in happy dreams; </div>
      
      <div class="l">The play of oars upon the flashing main, </div>
      
      <div class="l">The speed of runners, and the swelling vein, </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">And toil in pleasant upland field that teems </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">With vine and gadding gourd—until he seems </div>
      
      <div class="l">To feel wan memories of the sun again </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">And scent the vineyard slopes when dawn is wet, </div>
      
      <div class="l">But feels no ache within his loosened knees </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">To join the runners where the course is set, </div>
      
      <div class="l">Nor smite the billows of the fruitless seas,— </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">So I recall our day of passion yet, </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">With sighs and tenderness, but no regret. </div>

         </div>
         
         <div class="l">Denying all that nights of summer spake

      <h4>THE TAVERN</h4>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">In the tavern of my heart </div>
         
         <div class="l indented1">Many a one has sat before, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Drunk red wine and sung a stave, </div>
         
         <div class="l indented1">And, departing, come no more. </div>
         
         <div class="l">When the night was cold without </div>
         
         <div class="l indented1">And the ravens croaked of storm, </div>
         
         <div class="l">They have sat them at my hearth, </div>
         
         <div class="l indented1">Telling me my house was warm. </div>
          
      </div>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">As the lute and cup went round, </div>
         
         <div class="l indented1">They have rhymed me well in lay;— </div>
         
         <div class="l">When the hunt was on at morn, </div>
         
         <div class="l indented1">Each, departing, went his way. </div>
         
         <div class="l">On the walls, in compliment, </div>
         
         <div class="l indented1">Some would scrawl a verse or two, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Some have hung a willow branch, </div>
         
         <div class="l indented1">Or a wreath of corn flowers blue. </div>
          
      </div>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">Ah! my friend, when thou dost go, </div>
         
         <div class="l indented1">Leave no wreath of flowers for me; </div>
         
         <div class="l">Not pale daffodils nor rue, </div>
         
         <div class="l indented1">Violets nor rosemary. </div>
         
         <div class="l">Spill the wine upon the lamps, </div>
         
         <div class="l indented1">Tread the fire, and bar the door; </div>
         
         <div class="l">So defile the wretched place </div>
         
         <div class="l indented1">None will come, forevermore. </div>
          
      </div>

   <div class="lg poem">
      
      <h4>ANTINOUS</h4>
      
      <div class="l">With attributes of gods they sculptured him, </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">Hermes, Osiris, but were never wise </div>
      
      <div class="l">To lift the level, frowning brow of him </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">Or dull the mortal misery in his eyes; </div>
      
      <div class="l">The scornful weariness of every limb, </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">The dust begotten doubt that never dies, </div>
      
      <div class="l">Antinous, beneath thy lids, though dim, </div>
      
      <div class="l">The curling smoke of alters rose to thee, </div>
      
      <div class="l">Conjuring thee to comfort and content. </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">An emperor sent his galleys wide and far </div>
      
      <div class="l">To seek thy healing for thee.  Yea, and spent </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">Honor and treasure and red fruits of war </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">To lift thy heaviness, least thou should'st mar </div>
      
      <div class="l">The head that was an empire's glory, bent </div>
      
      <div class="l">A little, as the heavy poppies are. </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">Did the perfection of thy beauty pain </div>
      
      <div class="l">Thy limbs to bear it?  Did it ache to be, </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">As song hath ached in men, or passion vain? </div>
      
      <div class="l">Or lay it like some heavy robe on thee? </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">Was thy sick soul drawn from thee like the rain, </div>
      
      <div class="l">Or drunk up as the dead are drunk, each hour </div>
      
      <div class="l">To feed the color of some tulip flower? </div>

      <h4>PARADOX</h4>
      
      <div class="l">I knew them both upon Miranda's isle, </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">Which is of youth a sea-bound seigniory: </div>
      
      <div class="l">Misshapen Caliban, so seeming vile, </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">And Ariel, proud prince of mintrelsy, </div>
      
      <div class="l">Who did forsake the sunset for my tower </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">And like a star above my slumber burned. </div>
      
      <div class="l">The night was held in silver chains by power </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">Of melody, in which all longings yearned— </div>
      
      <div class="l">Star-grasping youth in one wild strain expressed, </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">Tender as dawn, insistant as the tide; </div>
      
      <div class="l">The heart of night and summer stood confessed. </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">I rose aglow and flung the lattice wide— </div>
      
      <div class="l">Ah jest of art, what mockery and pang! </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">Alack, it was poor Caliban who sang. </div>

      <div class="lg stanza">

         <div class="l indented1">Wild is the tempest crying, </div>

         <div class="l indented1">But Apollo, the god, Apollo? </div>

         <div class="l indented1">The watch that forever is keeping. </div>

         <div class="l indented1">Deep lies the snow on my pillow. </div>

         <div class="l indented1">As the nest forgot of the swallow: </div>

      <h4>ON CYNDUS</h4>
      
      <div class="l">The dream of all the world was at his feet: </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">Her eyes were heavy with the night of fate, </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">When, from the purple couch whereon she sate, </div>
      
      <div class="l">She rose, and took a jewel that was meet </div>
      
      <div class="l">For a queen's breast, where royal pulses beat— </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">A milk-white pearl, her milk-white bosom's mate, </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">Dropped in the golden chalice at his plate, </div>
      
      <div class="l">And to his lips held up the nectar sweet </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">And bade him drink the cup of destiny. </div>
      
      <div class="l">How shall he pledge again? by what emprise </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">A chalice find that holds a kingdom's fee? </div>
      
      <div class="l">Perchance in that charmed liquor he decries </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">A madman, raving while his galleys flee, </div>
      
      <div class="l indented1">Who casts a world into the wine-dark sea. </div>

   <div class="lg poem">
      
      <h4>THE NAMESAKE</h4>
      
      <h4>TO W.S.B., OF THE THIRTY-THIRD VIRGINIA</h4>
      
      <h4>
         
         <em>Vigesimum post annum in obscurum correpto lucem
            vigesimi
            <br></br>gaudens percipisse
         </em>
         
      </h4>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">Two by two and three by three </div>
         
         <div class="l">Missouri lies by Tennessee; </div>
         
         <div class="l">Row on row, and hundred deep, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Maryland and Georgia sleep; </div>
         
         <div class="l">Wistfully the poplars sigh </div>
         
         <div class="l">Where Virginia's thousands lie. </div>
          
      </div>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">Somewhere there among the stones, </div>
         
         <div class="l">All alike, that mark their bones, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Lies a lad beneath the pine </div>
         
         <div class="l">Who once bore a name like mine,— </div>
         
         <div class="l">Flung his splendid life away </div>
         
         <div class="l">Long before I saw the day. </div>
          
      </div>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">Often have they told me how </div>
         
         <div class="l">Hair like mine grew on his brow. </div>
         
         <div class="l">He was twenty to a day </div>
         
         <div class="l">When he got his jacket gray— </div>
         
         <div class="l">He was barely twenty-one </div>
         
         <div class="l">When they found him by his gun. </div>
          
      </div>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">Tell me, Uncle by the pine, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Had you such a girl as mine, </div>
         
         <div class="l">When you put her arms away </div>
         
         <div class="l">Riding to the wars that day? </div>
         
         <span class="hr"> </span>
         <span class="pageimage">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/large/cat.0005.033.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/large/cat.0005.033.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;">
               <img src="http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/thumbnails/cat.0005.033.jpg" class="display"></img>
            </a>
         </span>
         
         <div class="l">Were her lips so cold, instead </div>
         
         <div class="l">You must need to kiss the lead? </div>
          
      </div>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">Had the bugle, lilting gay, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Sweeter things than she to say? </div>
         
         <div class="l">Were there no gay fellows then, </div>
         
         <div class="l">You must seek these silent men? </div>
         
         <div class="l">Was your luck so bad at play </div>
         
         <div class="l">You must game your bones away? </div>
          
      </div>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">Ah! you lad with hair like mine, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Sleeping by the Georgia pine, </div>
         
         <div class="l">I'd be quick to quit the sun </div>
         
         <div class="l">Just to help you hold your gun, </div>
         
         <div class="l">And I'd leave my girl to share </div>
         
         <div class="l">Your still bed of glory there. </div>
          
      </div>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">Proud it is I am to know </div>
         
         <div class="l">In my veins there still must flow, </div>
         
         <div class="l">There to burn and bite away, </div>
         
         <div class="l">That proud blood you threw away; </div>
         
         <div class="l">And I'll be winner at the game </div>
         
         <div class="l">Enough for two who bore the name. </div>
          
      </div>

      <h4>LAMENT FOR MARSYAS
         
      </h4>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">Marsyas sleeps.  Oh, never wait, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Maidens, by the city gate, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Till he come to plunder gold </div>
         
         <div class="l">Of the daffodils you hold, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Or your branches white with May; </div>
         
         <div class="l">He is whiter gone than they. </div>
         
         <div class="l">He will startle you no more </div>
         
         <div class="l">When along the river shore </div>
         
         <div class="l">Damsels beat the linen clean. </div>
         
         <div class="l">Nor when maidens play at ball </div>
         
         <div class="l">Will he catch it where it fall: </div>
         
         <div class="l">Though ye wait for him and call </div>
         
         <div class="l">He will answer not, I ween. </div>
          
      </div>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">

         <div class="l">All who loved where powerless. </div>
         
         <div class="l">Ah! for that too-lovely head, </div>

         <div class="l">Many a yellow jonquil brim, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Many a hyacinth dewey-dim, </div>

      <h4>FROM THE VALLEY</h4>
      
      <div class="l">Toward the heights the pines climb row on row, </div>
      
      <div class="l">Processional guardians of the vestal snow, </div>
      
      <div class="l">Steel clad and somber, with their lances set </div>
      
      <div class="l">Against my heart, and like an old regret </div>
      
      <div class="l">For life unlived and love that could not be </div>
      
      <div class="l">Fall darkness, and the mountain's mystery. </div>
      
      <div class="l">Between the peaks, with line inviolate, </div>
      
      <div class="l">The arch of some vast wreck of Titan state </div>
      
      <div class="l">Yawns wide and cold, and empty galleries lie </div>
      
      <div class="l">Reverberating silence like a cry. </div>
      
      <div class="l">From ledges lifting skyward, tier on tier, </div>
      
      <div class="l">The elder gods, implacable, austere, </div>
      
      <div class="l">In their imperishable seats and high, </div>
      
      <div class="l">Behold the valley where our days go by </div>
      
      <div class="l">Like shining water, coming not again. </div>
      
      <div class="l">The solemn ministries of love and pain, </div>
      
      <div class="l">The runner fallen and the strong brought low, </div>
      
      <div class="l">And hands bound hard by sins of long ago. </div>

      <div class="l">They drop the misty curtains of the rain,— </div>

      <h4>PARIS</h4>
      
      <div class="l">Behind the arch of glory sets the day; </div>
      
      <div class="l">The river lies in curves of silver light, </div>
      
      <div class="l">The Fields Elysian glitter in a spray </div>
      
      <div class="l">Of golden dust; the gilded dome is bright, </div>
      
      <div class="l">The towers of Notre Dame cut clean and gray </div>
      
      <div class="l">The evening sky, and pale from left to right </div>
      
      <div class="l">A hundred bridges leap from either quay. </div>
      
      <div class="l">Pillared with pride, the city of delight </div>
      
      <div class="l">Sits like an empress by her silver Seine, </div>
      
      <div class="l">Heavy with jewels, all her splendid dower </div>
      
      <div class="l">Flashing upon her, won from shore and main </div>
      
      <div class="l">By shock of combat, sacked from town and tower. </div>
      
      <div class="l">Wherever men have builded hall or fane </div>
      
      <div class="l">Red war hath gleaned for her and men have slain </div>
      
      <div class="l">To deck her loveliness.  I feel again </div>
      
      <div class="l">That joy which brings her art to faultless flower, </div>
      
      <div class="l">That passion of her kings, who, reign on reign, </div>
      
      <div class="l">Arrayed her star by star with pride and power. </div>

   <div class="lg poem">
      
      <h4>SONG</h4>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">Troubador, when you were gay, </div>
         
         <div class="l">You wooed with rose and roundelay, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Singing harpstrings, sweet as May. </div>
         
         <div class="l">From beneath the crown of bay </div>
         
         <div class="l">Fell the wild, abundant hair. </div>
         
         <div class="l">Scent of cherry bloom and pear </div>
         
         <div class="l">With you from the south did fare, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Buds of myrtle for your wear. </div>
         
         <div class="l">Soft as summer stars thine eyes, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Planets pale in violet skies; </div>
         
         <div class="l">Summer wind that sings and dies </div>
         
         <div class="l">Was the music of thy sighs. </div>
          
      </div>
      
      <div class="lg stanza">
         
         <div class="l">Troubador, one winter's night, </div>
         
         <div class="l">When the pasture lands were white </div>
         
         <div class="l">And the cruel stars were bright, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Fortune held thee in despite. </div>
         
         <div class="l">Then beneath my tower you bore </div>
         
         <div class="l">Rose nor rondel as of yore, </div>
         
         <div class="l">But a heavy grief and sore </div>
         
         <div class="l">Laid in silence at my door. </div>
         
         <div class="l">April yearneth, April goes; </div>
         
         <div class="l">Not for me her violet blows, </div>
         
         <div class="l">I have done for long with those. </div>
         
         <div class="l">At my breast thy sorrow grows, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Nearer to my heart, God knows, </div>
         
         <div class="l">Than ever roundelay or rose! </div>
          
      </div>
       
   </div>

   <div class="lg poem">
      
      <h4>L'ENVOI</h4>
      
      <div class="l">Where are the loves that we had loved before </div>
      
      <div class="l">When once we are alone, and shut the door? </div>
      
      <div class="l">No matter whose the arms that held me fast, </div>
      
      <div class="l">The arms of Darkness hold me at the last. </div>
      
      <div class="l">No matter down what primrose path I tend, </div>
      
      <div class="l">I kiss the lips of Silence in the end. </div>
      
      <div class="l">No matter on what heart I found delight, </div>
      
      <div class="l">I come again unto the breast of Night. </div>
      
      <div class="l">No matter when or how love did befall, </div>
      
      <div class="l">'Tis Loneliness that loves me best of all, </div>
      
      <div class="l">And in the end she claims me, and I know </div>
      
      <div class="l">That she will stay, though all the rest may go. </div>
      
      <div class="l">No matter whose the eyes that I would keep </div>
      
      <div class="l">Near in the dark, 'tis in the eyes of Sleep </div>
      
      <div class="l">That I must look and look forever more, </div>
      
      <div class="l">When once I am alone, and shut the door. </div>
