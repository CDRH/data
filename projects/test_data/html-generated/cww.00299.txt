      <h3>PETITION.</h3>
      
      <p>To the Commissioners under the act of Congress approved the 16th of April, 1862, entitled "An act for the
         release of certain persons held to service or labor in the District of
         Columbia."
      </p>
      
      
      <p>Your Petitioners, 
         <span class="handwritten">
            
            <span class="persName">Samuel Shreve</span>
            
         </span> of 
         <span class="handwritten">
            
            <span class="placeName">Washington City DC</span>
            
         </span> by this 
         <span class="handwritten">his</span> petition in writing,
         represents and states, that 
         <span class="handwritten">he</span> is a person loyal
         to the United States, who, at the time of the passage of the said act of
         Congress, held a claim to service or labor against 
         <span class="handwritten">Three
            
         </span> person
         <span class="handwritten">s</span> of African descent of the name of 
         <span class="handwritten">
            
            <span class="persName">William Ceaser</span>, 
            <span class="persName">Eveline Clark</span>
            and 
            <span class="persName">Isabel Brook</span>
            
         </span> for and during the life of said
         
         <span class="handwritten">Three persons</span> and that by said act of Congress
         said 
         <span class="handwritten">Persons were</span> 
         <del class="was">was</del> discharged and freed of and
         from all claim of your petitioner to such service or labor; that at the time of
         said discharge said 
         <span class="handwritten">said 
            <span class="persName">William Ceaser</span>
            
         </span> was of the
         age of 
         <span class="handwritten">32 years</span> and of the personal descriptions
         following:
         <span class="foot">
            <a href="#footn1" id="bodyn1">(1)</a>
         </span>
         
         
         <span class="handwritten">of Dark Brown color about 5 feet seven inches high and
            well built, being a male person $1200
         </span>
      </p>
      
      
      <div class="ab handwritten">
         <span class="handwritten">The said 
            <span class="persName">Eveline</span>
            was of Dark Brown color about 5 feet four inches high, well built being a
            female person and the said 
            <span class="persName">Eveline</span> was of the age 18
            years $800
         </span> 
      </div>
      
      <div class="ab handwritten">
         <span class="handwritten">The said 
            <span class="persName">Isabel Brook</span> was of Bright Mulatto
            color, about 3 feet 10 inches high, well built and of the age of Ten Years $400
         </span> 
      </div>
      
      <div class="ab handwritten">
         <span class="handwritten">(Note) I would offer as witnesses 
            <br></br>
            
            <span class="persName">Edward G. Donaldson</span>
            <br></br>
            
            <span class="persName">R. H. Williams</span>
            <br></br>
            of 
            <span class="placeName">Washington City DC</span>
         </span> 
      </div>
      
      
      <span class="hr"> </span>
      <span class="pageimage">
         <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/large/leaf001v.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/large/leaf001v.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;">
            <img src="http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/thumbnails/leaf001v.jpg" class="display"></img>
         </a>
      </span>
      
      
      <p>That your petitioner acquired 
         <span class="handwritten">his</span> claim to the
         aforesaid service or labor of said 
         <span class="handwritten">3. Persons</span> in
         manner following:
         <span class="foot">
            <a href="#footn2" id="bodyn2">(2)</a>
         </span>
         
         <span class="handwritten">I purchased them at the administrators sale of my father
            (
            <span class="persName">Sam Shreve</span>'s Estate) of 
            <span class="placeName">Montgomery
               County Maryland
            </span> in November
            1861.
         </span>
         
      </p>
      
      
      <p>That your petitioner's claim to the service or labor of said 
         <span class="handwritten">3. Persons</span> was, at the time of said discharge
         therefrom, of the value of 
         <span class="handwritten">Twenty four hundred</span>
         dollars in money.
         <span class="foot">
            <a href="#footn3" id="bodyn3">(3)</a>
         </span>
         
         <span class="handwritten">Your petitioners avers that the said persons were by
            
            <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="reason​ ">reson</a>
            ​ of their intelligence, honesty and Physical ability very valuable to
            him. That the said 
            <span class="persName">Williams</span> wages at the time of the
            passage of the act of Congress was of the value of one dollar a day which I
            had been for some time receiving for him. I now allow
            
            <span class="persName">Eveline</span> 8 
            <a rel="tooltip" class="abbr" title="dollars​ ">dolls</a>​ per Month, and
            
            <span class="persName">Isabel</span> two 
            <a rel="tooltip" class="abbr" title="dollars​ ">dolls</a>​ per month and victuals and clothes
            and your petitioner has no knowledge of any moral mental or bodily
            infirmities or defects that would impair the value of said three
            persons
         </span>
         
      </p>
      
      
      <p>Your petitioner hereby declares that 
         <span class="handwritten">he</span> bears true
         and faithful allegiance to the Government of the United States, and that 
         <span class="handwritten">he</span> has not borne arms against the United States in
         the present rebellion, nor in any way given aid or comfort thereto.
      </p>
      
      
      <p>And your petitioner further states and alleges, that 
         <span class="handwritten">he</span> has not brought said 
         <span class="handwritten">persons</span> into
         the 
         <span class="placeName">District of Columbia</span> since the passage of said act of
         Congress; and that, at the time of the passage thereof, said 
         <span class="handwritten">persons</span> was held to service or labor therein under
         and by virtue of your petitioner's claim to such service or labor.
      </p>
      
      
      <p>Your petitioners further state and allege, that 
         <span class="handwritten">his</span>
         said claim to the service or labor of said 
         <span class="handwritten">persons
            
         </span> does not originate in or by virtue of any transfer heretofore made by any
         person who has in any manner aided or sustained the present rebellion against
         the Government of the United States.
      </p>
      
      
      <p>And your petitioners pray the said Commissioners to investigate and determine the
         validity of 
         <span class="handwritten">his</span> said claim to the service or labor
         of said 
         <span class="handwritten">persons</span> herein above set forth; and if the
         same be found to be valid, that they appraise and apportion the value of said
         claim in money, and report the same to the Secretary of the Treasury of the
         United States, in conformity to the provisions of said act of Congress.
      </p>
      
      
      <div class="closer"> (Signed by) 
         <br></br>
         
         <span class="handwritten">
            
            <span class="persName">Samuel Shreve</span>
            
         </span>
         
          
      </div>
