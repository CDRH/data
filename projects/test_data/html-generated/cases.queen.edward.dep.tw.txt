      <h3>
         <span class="persName">Edward Queen</span>
         <br></br>
         v
         <br></br>
         The Revd 
         <span class="persName">John Ashton</span>
         <br></br>
         ) Petition for freedom in the General Court
      </h3>
      
      
      <p>The deposition of 
         <span class="persName">Thomas Warfield</span> aged forty two years or
         thereabouts, being sworn on the holy Evangelity of Almighty God saith, that
         about the year seventeen hundred and eighty two, or seventeen hundred &amp;
         eighty three this deponent was overseer at Bright Seat in 
         <span class="placeName">Anne
            Arundel County
         </span> for the late Reverend 
         <span class="persName">
            <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="James Carroll​ ">John
               Carrick
            </a>
            ​
         </span>
         and had carried down six Hogsheads of Tobacco for the said 
         <span class="persName">
            <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="James Carroll​ ">John
               Carrick
            </a>
            ​
         </span> to Taylor's Landing Warehouse
         where Capt. 
         <span class="persName">John Jiams</span>, now deceased, was Inspector, and
         the said Inspector was going to mark the tobacco with the name of the deponent
         when the deponent told him not, but to mark it with the name of 
         <span class="persName">
            <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="James Carroll​ ">John
               Carrick
            </a>
            ​
         </span>,
         priest, upon
         which the said Capt. 
         <span class="persName">John Jiams</span> then asked the deponent if
         he knew old 
         <span class="persName">Phillis</span> who lived at the quarter, to which he
         answered that he did know her very well; the deponent then asked the said
         
         <span class="persName">John Jiams</span> if he knew her, and he answered he knew her very
         well and said she was as free as he was if she had her right; the deponent then
         asked him how she came to be free, and he answered that 
         <del class="cc">cc</del> her mother was
         free and was brought into this Country by Captain 
         <span class="persName">Larkin</span>
         and was sold for seven years, and 
         <del class="was to [illegible] by [illegible] Chapman">was to  by  Chapman</del> the deponent thinks
         the said 
         <span class="persName">Jiams</span> told him that she was sold to one of the
         
         <span class="persName">Chapman</span>s; and he further says that the said Captain
         
         <span class="persName">Jiams</span> told him that her name was 
         <span class="persName">Mary
            Queen
         </span>, to the best of his recollection; and that she afterwards
         belonged to 
         <span class="persName">James 
            <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="Carroll​ ">Carrick</a>
            ​
         </span> who lived at 
         <span class="placeName">Fingal</span> in 
         <span class="placeName">Ann
            Arundel County
         </span>, and who as this deponent has understood left the
         place aforementioned
      </p>

      <p>together with the 
         <span class="placeName">White Marsh plantation</span> and his personal
         Estate to the Church; being asked how far Captain 
         <span class="persName">John
            Jiams
         </span> lived from the quarter where 
         <span class="persName">Phillis</span>
         then lived, answered about a mile, and he always lived there ever since the
         memory of this deponent; and he has always understood that
         
         <span class="persName">Phillis</span> aforementioned is the mother of 
         <span class="persName">Ned</span> the petitioner. Being asked what is the
         Complexion of 
         <span class="persName">Phillis</span>, answered she is yellow and appears
         to be a bright mulatto: and further saith not.
      </p>
      
      
      <p>Sworn to before the subscriber this 11th Octr
         1792
      </p>
