   <h3>Call for Papers on Willa Cather:
      <br></br>  American Literature Association
      2013, May 23-26, Boston, MA
   </h3>
   
   
   
   
   
   <p>The Willa Cather Foundation soliciting the best of ongoing Cather scholarship for
      presentation (as 20-minute papers) at the annual meeting of the American Literature
      Association in Boston, Massachusetts, May 23-26, 2013.
   </p>
   
   
   <p>As we have done for the last few years, we will accept proposals for all approaches
      to
      Cather's work, including but not limited to
      
      
      <ul>
         <li>studies of Cather's short stories, poetry, or "extra-literary" work (journalism,
            letters, travel sketches, essays, etc.);
         </li>
         
         <li>reconsiderations of Cather's various classifications as historical, psychological,
            or
            philosophical novelist; as a regional writer; as a modernist; etc.;
         </li>
         
         <li>identification of Cather's textual relations with other writers;</li>
         
         <li>explorations of Cather's politics, conscious and unconscious;</li>
         
         <li>formal and rhetorical analyses of Cather's work;</li> 
         <li>and
            new biographical research.
         </li>
      </ul>
      
   </p>
   
   <p>We will select presentations that promise to represent the best and most innovative
      of
      current Cather scholarship.
   </p>
   
   
   <p>Please email inquiries or a proposal of about 300 words (in Microsoft Word) to John
      Swift at  
      <a href="mailto:swiftj@oxy.edu" id="mailto:swiftj@oxy.edu.ref" class="footnote">swiftj@oxy.edu</a>  (ECLS Department, Occidental College, Los Angeles CA
      90041). The deadline for proposals will be Friday, January 4th, but they will be
      welcomed earlier. Our panel proposal must be submitted by the end of January, as
      described in the conference's official announcement:
   </p>
   
   
   <p>The American Literature Association's 24th annual conference will meet at the Westin
      Copley Place in Boston on May 23-26, 2013 (Thursday through Sunday of Memorial
      Day weekend). The deadline for proposals is January 30, 2013. For further information,
      please consult the ALA website at  
      <a href="http://www.americanliterature.org" id="http://www.americanliterature.org.ref" class="footnote">www.americanliterature.org</a>  or contact the conference
      director, Professor Olivia Carr Edenfield via email ( 
      <a href="mailto:carr@georgiasouthern.edu" id="mailto:carr@georgiasouthern.edu.ref" class="footnote">carr@georgiasouthern.edu</a> ) with
      specific questions.
   </p>
