      <h3>PETITION.</h3>
      
      <p>To the Commissioners under the act of Congress approved the 16th of April, 1862, entitled "An act for the
         release of certain persons held to service or labor in the District of
         Columbia."
      </p>
      
      
      <p>Your Petitioner, 
         <span class="handwritten">
            
            <span class="persName">Benjamin K. Morsell</span>
            
         </span> of 
         <span class="handwritten">
            
            <span class="placeName">Maryland</span>
            
         </span> by this 
         <span class="handwritten">his</span> petition in writing, represents
         and states, that 
         <span class="handwritten">he</span> is a person loyal to the
         United States, who, at the time of the passage of the said act of Congress, held
         a claim to service or labor against 
         <span class="handwritten">a person discharged
            therefrom by said act a
         </span> person of African descent of the name of 
         <span class="handwritten">
            
            <span class="persName">William Dodson</span>
            
         </span> for and during the life of said 
         <span class="handwritten">
            
            <span class="persName">William Dodson</span>
            
         </span> and that by said act of Congress said 
         <span class="handwritten">William
            Dodson
         </span> was discharged and freed of and from all claim of your
         petitioner to such service or labor; that at the time of said discharge said
         
         <span class="handwritten">William Dodson</span> was of the age of 
         <span class="handwritten">thirty two years</span> and of the personal description
         following:
         <span class="foot">
            <a href="#footn1" id="bodyn1">(1)</a>
         </span>
         
         <span class="handwritten">a tall Black man strong and healthy, free from disease
            so far as 
            <span class="illegible">[his?]</span>
            knowledge extends. His height is, probably, from five feet, ten inches, to
            six feet. He is of spare build and his impression is that he will weigh 150
            or 160 lbs or thereabouts.
         </span>
         
      </p>
      
      
      <span class="hr"> </span>
      <span class="pageimage">
         <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/large/leaf001v.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/large/leaf001v.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;">
            <img src="http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/thumbnails/leaf001v.jpg" class="display"></img>
         </a>
      </span>
      
      
      <p>That your petitioner acquired 
         <span class="handwritten">his</span> claim to the
         aforesaid service or labor of said 
         <span class="handwritten">William Dodson</span>
         in manner following:
         <span class="foot">
            <a href="#footn2" id="bodyn2">(2)</a>
         </span>
         
         <span class="handwritten"> He was born his slave his mother being his property at
            the time of his birth. He was born in the 
            <span class="placeName">city of
               Washington
            </span> in the 
            <span class="placeName">District of
               Columbia
            </span>, and has resided in said city continually from that
            time to the present. 
         </span>
         
      </p>
      
      
      <p>That your petitioner
         <del class="'s">'s</del>
         
         <span class="handwritten">belies as his</span> claim to the service or labor of
         said 
         <span class="handwritten">
            
            <span class="persName">William Dodson</span>
            
         </span> was, at the time of said discharge therefrom, of the value of 
         <span class="handwritten">one thousand</span> dollars in money.
         <span class="foot">
            <a href="#footn3" id="bodyn3">(3)</a>
         </span>
         
         <span class="handwritten">He has always been strictly honest and perfectly
            trustworthy. He is also a sober and industrious man and, you petitioner
            believes, a professor of religion. He has been in the employ of different
            brick layers for some fifteen or sixteen years, and though his usual
            occupation has been 
            <u>carrying the 
               <span class="illegible">[lead?]</span>
            </u> or 
            <u>cart-driving</u>, yet he has informed petitioner that
            he has sometimes been engaged in 
            <u>paving</u>.—He
            has done small jobs of 
            <span class="illegible">[cart driving?]</span> for petitioner. He is a generally useful man. In
            early life he 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> at Hotels as a dining room servant. He
            knows of no infirmities or defects of said 
            <span class="persName">Dodson</span>,
            moral, mental or bodily which impair the value of his claim to his service
            or labor
         </span>
         
      </p>
      
      
      <p>Your petitioner hereby declares that 
         <span class="handwritten">he</span> bears true
         and faithful allegiance to the Government of the United States, and that 
         <span class="handwritten">he</span> has not borne arms against the United States in
         the present rebellion, nor in any way given aid or comfort thereto.
      </p>
      
      
      <p>And your petitioner further states and alleges, that 
         <span class="handwritten">he</span> has not brought said 
         <span class="handwritten">"slave"</span> into
         the District of Columbia since the passage of said act of Congress; and that, at
         the time of the passage thereof, said 
         <span class="handwritten">"slave"</span> was
         held to service or labor therein under and by virtue of your petitioner's claim
         to such service or labor.
      </p>
      
      
      <p>Your petitioner further states and alleges, that 
         <span class="handwritten">his</span> said claim to the service or labor of said 
         <span class="handwritten">"slave"</span> does not originate in or by virtue of any
         transfer heretofore made by any person who has in any manner aided or sustained
         the present rebellion against the Government of the United States.
      </p>
      
      
      <p>And your petitioner prays the said Commissioners to investigate and determine the
         validity of 
         <span class="handwritten">his</span> said claim to the service or labor
         of said 
         <span class="handwritten">
            
            <span class="persName">William Dodson</span>
            
         </span> herein above set forth; and if the same be found to be valid, that they
         appraise and apportion the value of said claim in money, and report the same to
         the Secretary of the Treasury of the United States, in conformity to the
         provisions of said act of Congress.
      </p>
      
      
      <div class="closer"> (Signed by) 
         <br></br>
         
         <span class="handwritten">
            
            <span class="persName">B. K. Morsell</span>
            
         </span>
         
          
      </div>
