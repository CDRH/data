ST. LOUIS POST-DISPATCH

"OF MAKING MANY BOOKS"

JOHN G. NEIHARDT

BRANSON, MISSOURI
Sat. Eve.
[1931] ?[NOV 19, 193 [1?]?]?
Dear Comrade:

Here is another item in the case of Pattee against common Honesty:

If the gentleman, far from taking the trouble of reading my Cycle before rendering judgement thereon, has merely looked into the Drinkwater-Canby-Benet anthology of English poetry or, better still, into the Quinn-Baugh-Howe work, the Literature of America, he could have learned with little effort how foolish the professorial Greek-imitation charge is. These are only two anthologies (both standard) that he might have consulted.

Goddamn liars and fools!

With love always,


Jno.
