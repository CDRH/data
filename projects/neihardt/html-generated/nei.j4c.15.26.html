ST. LOUIS POST-DISPATCH

"OF MAKING MANY BOOKS"

JOHN G. NEIHARDT

BRANSON, MISSOURI
February 24 1 9 3 1
Dear Comrade:

You quote Edmund Wilson in your last, evidently believing that he is a man deserving the attention of an intelligent man like yourself. I am sending you a copy of the latest book by this pink tea lizard, that you may know more about him. You need read no more in this book than the study of Gertrude Stein. We have reached a point where you may take this as a safe axiom: Wherever there are any leaders in any realm of human activity, including the cultural realm, you will find fools more conspicuous. The simple fact is that western society is uncurably insane.

But that is an unpleasant matter. Of course I take your suggestion "at all seriously" as to rewriting your book, and I am genuinely interested, but for no personal reason as you might suppose. You could make a fine thing of that now, I am sure.

How good it is that Mary is so prosperously and happily married! This must give you much solid satisfaction when you think of it.

With love always,


Jno.
