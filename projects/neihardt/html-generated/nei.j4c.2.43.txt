<h6 class="fw">43</h6>

<span class="dateline">Aug 17, '14</span>
    
<span class="salute">My dear Mr. Sinclair:-</span>
    
    <p>In response to yours of the 10th inst., I would suggest the following poems from my last two volumes of verse:</p>
 
<ul><li><strong>In "Man-Song" (Kennerley) - </strong><br/>
    
    Czologosz. (page 34)</li><li><strong>In "The Stranger at the Gate" (Kennerley) - </strong><br/>
    
Money (page 63)</li><li>The Red Wind Comes (page 64)</li><li>The Cry of the People (page 66)</li></ul>
 
    <p>The sonnet "Money" seems to me to be especially significant.</p>
    
    <p>With best wishes,</p>
    
<span class="closer">
    <span class="salute">Fraternally,</span>
    
    <br/>Jno. G. Neihardt
</span>
