      <div class="note">10. Bowen letter</div>
      
      <h6 class="fw">7</h6>
      
      
      <div class="dateline">
         Skyrim Farm,
         Route 6,
         Columbia, Mo.
         
         Jan. 15, '55
          
      </div>
      
      
      
      <div class="salute">Dear Dr. Bowen: </div>
      
      
      
      <p>Good to hear from you! Yes, that poem has had some adventures in its 30 years of life!
         I've even seen it with other names than mine attached — and feel flattered. One very
         old man came to think he wrote it himself. A sourdough in Alaska declared it saved
         his life — etc. etc.
      </p>
      
      
      <p>Use the second version.</p>
      
      <p>"Let me 
         <u>lie</u>" must be "Let me 
         
         <u>die</u>."
      </p>
      
      <p>"Soul-house" hyphenated.</p>
      
      <p>"Time-swept" is horrible! Make it "
         <u>"tune</u>-swept."
      </p>
      
      
      <p>Things go very well at the University. My </p>
      <p>
         <u>Epic America</u>
      </p>
      <p> course continues to be popular and classes grow larger and larger
         
      </p>
      <p>
         <span class="hr"> </span>
      </p>
      <h6 class="fw">8</h6>
      <h6 class="fw">-2-</h6>
      <p>
         
         in spite of stiff requirements. I teach two writing courses also. Lots of fun!
      </p>
      
      
      <p>It seems I'm to read poetry at the Michigan State College Speech Conference, June
         25-26. Perhaps I shall see you.
      </p>
      
      <div class="closer">
         
         <div class="salute">Kindest thoughts </div>
         
         <br></br>John G. Neihardt
          
      </div>

      <p>From THE QUEST (1928), page 50:</p>
      
      <div class="lg">
         
         <h3>LET ME LIVE OUT MY YEARS</h3>
         
         
         <div class="lg">
            <div class="l">Let me live out my years in heat of blood! </div>
            
            <div class="note">Correct</div>  
            <div class="l">Let me die drunken with the dreamer's wine! </div>
            
            <div class="l">Let me not see this soul-house built of mud </div>
            
            <div class="l">Go toppling to the dust--a vacant shrine! </div> 
         </div>
         
         
         <div class="lg">
            <div class="l">Let me go quickly like a candle light </div>
            
            <div class="l">Snuffed out just at the heyday of its glow! </div>
            
            <div class="l">Give me high noon--and let it then be night! </div>
            
            <div class="l">Thus would I go. </div> 
         </div>
         
         
         <div class="lg">
            <div class="l">And grant me, when I face the grisly Thing, </div>
            
            <div class="l">
               <u>One haughty cry to pierce the gray Perhaps!</u> 
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">Let me be as a 
               <u>tune</u>-swept fiddlestring 
            </div>
            
            <div class="l">That feels the Master Melody--and snaps! </div> 
         </div>
          
      </div>
      
      
      
      <p>From Argus Tresidder, READING TO OTHERS, Scott, Foresman, 1940, p. 348 (acknowledgement to Macmillan and THE QUEST):</p>
      
      
      <div class="lg">
