      <div class="dateline">January 27, 1939 </div>
      
      
      
      Mr. and Mrs. Elmer E. Holm
      Mariaville, Nebraska.
      
      
      
      <div class="salute">Dear friends: </div>
      
      
      <p>I'm sending the manuscript of "Straws". I hope you can read it at once, make your
         notes, and let me have it back 
         <u>within two weeks</u>. I must have some of the material for reference in preparing a week's seris of lectures
         before the Des Moines Forum.
      </p>
      
      
      <p>I've read the Garland brochure. Compared with yours - what a thin, perfunctory thing
         it is!
      </p>
      
      
      <p>Have you read Poetic Values? If not, you should I can send a copy.</p>
      
      
      <p>You are doing a real job. Be sure that we are sincerely grateful to you.</p>
      
      
      <p>I've been making astonishing progress with "Jed Smith" - 10 to 17 lines a day for
         some weeks! Am stopped now for some lectures; but, barring accident, I should finish
         in the late summer.
      </p>
      
      <div class="closer">
         
         <div class="salute">Kindest thoughts, </div>
         
         
         <br></br>Jno. G. Neihardt
         
         
         <br></br>John G. Neihardt.
