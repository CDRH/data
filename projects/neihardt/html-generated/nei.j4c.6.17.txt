      <div class="salute">Dear Sterling: — </div>
      
      
      	
      <p>A little while ago Mr. &amp; Mrs. Ralph Waldo Trine visiting here with us. Mrs. T. is
         a booster of poets, and I gave a letter of introduction to you.
      </p>
      	
      <p>That was a good letter from you, and I had a good chuckle over the "two murders and
         a rope"; but I knew damned well that the drama would be a gem. When am I to see it,
         Old Man?
      </p>
      	
      <p>I've just been given the $500 prize for 1919 — the Poetry Society  prize. Seems that
         Gladys Cromwell and I were a tie. Then poor Gladys died — or had died already — and
