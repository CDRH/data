      <div class="dateline">Monday. </div>
      
      
      <div class="salute">Dear Comrade: </div>
      
      
      <p>I couldn't get the MS off until today, which is pretty late. But I note by yours just
         received that you are going ahead and that what you are doing even be substituted
         for the Notes I am returning to you. Bully! But I think you should go over the MS
         I am sending, being careful to incorporate 
         <del class=""></del> anything that you may lack in the second version. There are some corrections as to
         fact.
      </p>
      
      
      <p>I was troubled yesterday when I wrote you, but now I know you will make an excellent
         job of the Notes. We are in high company when we enter the Modern Readers' Series
         - the 
         
      </p>
      <p>
         <span class="hr"> </span>
      </p>
      <h6 class="fw">72</h6>
      <p>
         highest there is, and too much attention can not be given to the Notes. The book is
         going to live on and on. Also, Ashley Thorndike of Columbia is general editor of the
         series. I would be on you if I knew your whole mind was on the job - every time. I
         feel that it 
      </p>
      <p>
         <em>is</em>
      </p>
      <p> all on the job now.</p>
      
      
      <p>Cut out any explanations that, upon second thought, seem obvious, that is, obvious
         to the sort of people we must reach. Enid will copy what you send back.
      </p>
      
      
      <p>And be sure to send me a bill for your time. That is the rest thing to do. 
         <div class="note">Make it enough to cover.</div>
      </p>
      
      <div class="closer">
         
         <div class="salute">Endless love, </div>
         
         
         <br></br>Jno
