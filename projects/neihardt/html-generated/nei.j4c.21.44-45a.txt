      <div class="dateline">2-25-'58 </div>
      
      
      
      <div class="salute">Dear Mrs. Aly: </div>
      
      
      <p>I'm at the office. Want to tell you about John Chaffee. He was not my companion on
         the trip down the Missouri in 1908. He came 10 years earlier. John had a stroke after
         his wife died last August, and his sister, Edna, writes his letters: Edna has an invalid
         husband, and I'm sure the situation is a bit saddening. John is looking forward to
         seeing you &amp; Dr. Aly.
      </p>
      
      
      <span class="hr"> </span>

      <h6 class="fw">45</h6>
      
      <h6 class="fw">-2-</h6>
      
      
      <p>My boy companion on the trip down in 1908 was Chet Marshal, dead long ago.</p>
      
      
      <p>I hope you can cheer John up. His wife's death seems to have broken him.</p>
      
      <div class="closer">
         
         <div class="salute">Kindest thoughts </div>
         
         <br></br>Jno. N.
          
      </div>
      
      
      <p>Did I tell you I'm going to Cornell again in May?</p>
