                <span class="salute">Dear Mrs. Aly:</span>
                
                <p>I'm at the office. Want to tell you about John Chaffee. He was not my companion on the trip down the Missouri in 1908. He came 10 years earlier. John had a stroke after his wife died last August, and his sister, Edna, writes his letters: Edna has an invalid husband, and I'm sure the situation is a bit saddening. John is looking forward to seeing you &amp; Dr. Aly.</p>
                    
                    <span class="hr"> </span><span class="pageimage"><a href="large/nei.j4c.21.44-45a_00002.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;large/nei.j4c.21.44-45a_00002.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;"><img src="thumbnails/nei.j4c.21.44-45a_00002.jpg" class="display &#xA;            "/></a></span>
                    <h6 class="fw">45</h6>
                   <h6 class="fw">-2-</h6>
                    
                    <p>My boy companion on the trip down in 1908 was Chet Marshal, dead long ago.</p>
                
                <p>I hope you can cheer John up. His wife's death seems to have broken him.</p>
                <span class="closer">
                <span class="salute">Kindest thoughts</span>
                <br/>Jno. N.
                </span>
                
                <p>Did I tell you I'm going to Cornell again in May?</p>
